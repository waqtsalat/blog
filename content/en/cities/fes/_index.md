# Prayer Times in Fès

Fès, often spelled Fez in English, is considered the spiritual and intellectual heart of Morocco. Home to the University of Al-Qarawiyyin, recognized by UNESCO and Guinness World Records as the oldest continuously operating university in the world, the city has been a center of Islamic learning for over 1,200 years. Prayer times in Fès carry special significance given the city's scholarly heritage.

## Daily Prayer Schedule

Fès prayer times follow Morocco's official Habous Ministry method:

| Prayer | Calculation | Time Variation |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Fajr** | 19° angle | 3:50 AM - 6:20 AM |
| **Chourouk** | Sunrise | 5:20 AM - 7:45 AM |
| **Dhuhr** | Solar noon | 12:05 PM - 12:35 PM |
| **Asr** | Shafi'i method | 3:15 PM - 4:30 PM |
| **Maghrib** | Sunset | 5:35 PM - 7:50 PM |
| **Isha** | 17° angle | 7:05 PM - 9:20 PM |

Fès' inland valley location provides clear conditions for astronomical observations used in prayer time calculations.

## Al-Qarawiyyin Mosque and University

The Al-Qarawiyyin complex represents the pinnacle of Islamic scholarship:

- **Founded:** 859 CE by Fatima al-Fihri
- **Status:** Oldest existing university in the world
- **Library:** Contains some of the oldest manuscripts in existence
- **Architecture:** Andalusian influence with stunning courtyards

The mosque's prayer times have been meticulously recorded for centuries, and the institution continues to be an important religious center for Morocco.

## Historic Mosques of Fès

### Al-Andalus Mosque
The second major mosque of Fès, built in the Andalusian style, serving the western quarter of the medina.

### Bou Inania Madrasa
A masterpiece of Marinid architecture, this religious school includes an active mosque and is one of the few religious sites in Morocco open to non-Muslim visitors.

### Zaouia Moulay Idriss II
The shrine of the founder of Fès and descendant of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), an important pilgrimage site and active mosque.

### Mosquée R'cif
A major congregational mosque in the heart of the medina, serving thousands of worshippers for daily prayers.

## Fès' Religious Significance

**Scholarly Tradition:** Fès has produced countless Islamic scholars over the centuries. The precise calculation of prayer times has been a subject of study and refinement here since the city's founding.

**Imperial Capital:** As the first imperial city of Morocco, Fès established many of the religious practices that became standard across the country.

**Medina Preservation:** The entire Fès el-Bali medina is a UNESCO World Heritage site, with prayer times echoing through ancient streets much as they have for a millennium.
