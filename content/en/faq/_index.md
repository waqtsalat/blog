# Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about WaqtSalat, prayer time calculations, and using the app effectively.

## Prayer Times Calculation

### What calculation method does WaqtSalat use?

WaqtSalat uses the **Habous Ministry method**, which is the official prayer time calculation for Morocco:

- **Fajr angle:** 19° (sun below horizon)
- **Isha angle:** 17° (sun below horizon)
- **Asr:** Shafi'i method

This matches the times announced at local mosques throughout Morocco.

### Why do different apps show different prayer times?

Many prayer apps use calculation methods designed for other countries:

| Method | Region | Fajr | Isha |
|--------|--------|------|------|
| **Habous** | Morocco | 19° | 17° |
| Umm al-Qura | Saudi Arabia | 18.5° | Fixed |
| MWL | International | 18° | 17° |
| Egyptian | Egypt | 19.5° | 17.5° |

WaqtSalat is specifically designed for Morocco and uses the local Habous method.

### Why does Morocco display six prayer times instead of five?

Morocco includes **Chourouk (sunrise)** alongside the five daily prayers:

1. Fajr - Dawn prayer
2. **Chourouk - Sunrise (not a prayer)**
3. Dhuhr - Noon prayer
4. Asr - Afternoon prayer
5. Maghrib - Sunset prayer
6. Isha - Night prayer

Chourouk marks the end time for Fajr prayer. You cannot pray Fajr after Chourouk.

### How accurate are WaqtSalat's calculations?

WaqtSalat calculates times to within seconds based on:
- Exact city coordinates
- Morocco's timezone (GMT+0/GMT+1)
- Official Habous Ministry angles

Times match what you hear from local mosques.

## Using the App

### Does WaqtSalat work offline?

**Yes!** WaqtSalat works completely offline after initial setup:

1. Download the app
2. Select your city (stored locally)
3. Prayer times are calculated on your device
4. No internet needed for daily use

This is ideal for travel, areas with poor connectivity, or avoiding data usage.

### Can I adjust prayer times to match my local mosque?

Absolutely. WaqtSalat allows **±15 minute adjustments** for each prayer:

- Open Settings
- Select the prayer to adjust
- Move the slider to match your mosque

This accounts for local variations in Adhan timing.

### How do I enable Adhan notifications?

1. Open WaqtSalat
2. Go to Settings → Notifications
3. Enable "Adhan at prayer time"
4. Choose your preferred reciter

You'll hear the call to prayer at each prayer time.

### Which cities are supported?

WaqtSalat supports **35+ Moroccan cities**, including:
- Rabat, Casablanca, Marrakech, Fès, Tangier, Agadir
- Meknès, Oujda, Kenitra, Tétouan, Safi
- El Jadida, Beni Mellal, Nador, and many more

Select your city from the list for accurate local times.

**No.** WaqtSalat is designed with privacy as a priority:

**Never.** WaqtSalat is completely ad-free:

- No banner ads

## Technical Questions

### What devices support WaqtSalat?

WaqtSalat is a **Progressive Web App (PWA)** that works on:

- **Android:** Full support with Adhan notifications
- **iOS:** Full support (install via Safari)
- **Desktop:** Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari

Install it like a native app from your browser.

### How do I install WaqtSalat on my phone?

**Android:**
1. Visit waqtsalat.github.io/waqtsalat in Chrome
2. Tap "Add to Home Screen"
3. The app icon appears on your home screen

**iOS:**
1. Visit waqtsalat.github.io/waqtsalat in Safari
2. Tap the Share button
3. Select "Add to Home Screen"

### Why does Isha time seem late/early?

Isha time varies significantly throughout the year:

- **Summer:** Isha can be after 10 PM (sunset is late, and Isha requires 17° below horizon)
- **Winter:** Isha is around 8 PM (earlier sunset)

This is correct per the Habous method and matches mosque times.

### Can I use WaqtSalat in other countries?

WaqtSalat is optimized for Morocco using the Habous method. While you can manually adjust times, the calculation angles are specific to Morocco.

For other countries, consider apps that support multiple calculation methods.
