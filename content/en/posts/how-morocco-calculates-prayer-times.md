Different prayer apps show different times. This creates confusion about when to actually pray. Morocco uses a specific calculation method defined by the Habous Ministry.

## What Is the Habous Method?

The Habous method is Morocco's official prayer time calculation. It uses specific sun angles to determine Fajr and Isha times.

**Key angles:**
- **Fajr**: 19° (sun below horizon)
- **Isha**: 17° (sun below horizon)

These angles are more conservative than other methods, resulting in slightly earlier Fajr and later Isha times.

## Why Different Apps Show Different Times

Many apps default to calculation methods used in other countries:

- **Umm al-Qura** (Saudi Arabia): Fajr 18.5°, Isha uses fixed time after Maghrib
- **Muslim World League**: Fajr 18°, Isha 17°
- **Egyptian General Authority**: Fajr 19.5°, Isha 17.5°

## The Six Daily Prayer Times

Morocco displays six prayer times instead of the usual five:

1. **Fajr** - Dawn prayer
2. **Chourouk** - Sunrise (not a prayer, but important marker)
3. **Dhuhr** - Noon prayer
4. **Asr** - Afternoon prayer
5. **Maghrib** - Sunset prayer
6. **Isha** - Night prayer

Chourouk marks when the sun fully rises. You cannot pray Fajr after Chourouk.
