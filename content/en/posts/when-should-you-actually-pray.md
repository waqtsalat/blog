The athan rings. Do you pray immediately or wait? This question comes up often, especially when different people in the same household pray at different times.

## The Short Answer

You can pray anytime between the athan and the next prayer's athan. There's a full window for each prayer, not a single moment.

## Understanding Prayer Windows

Each prayer has a defined time window:

- **Fajr**: From Fajr athan until sunrise (Chourouk)
- **Dhuhr**: From Dhuhr athan until Asr athan
- **Asr**: From Asr athan until Maghrib athan
- **Maghrib**: From Maghrib athan until Isha athan
- **Isha**: From Isha athan until Fajr athan

Within these windows, you have flexibility. The prophet (peace be upon him) encouraged praying early in the window, but valid reasons exist to delay.

## Why People Wait

You might notice some people wait 10-20 minutes after athan before praying. Common reasons:

**For Fajr:**
- Waiting for complete darkness to fade
- Joining family members
- Completing sunnah prayers first

**For Dhuhr:**
- Waiting for the sun to pass zenith completely
- Work or school schedules
- Waiting for the mosque congregation

**For Asr:**
- The prophet recommended praying Asr early
- But delaying slightly is valid if needed

**For Maghrib:**
- Usually prayed immediately (shortest window)
- Breaking fast first during Ramadan

**For Isha:**
- Some wait until complete darkness
- Delaying until one-third of the night is acceptable

## Preferred Times vs. Valid Times

**Preferred (Sunnah):** Pray early in the window, soon after athan.

**Valid:** Anytime within the window.

**Disliked (Makruh):** Delaying until very late in the window without reason.

**Invalid:** Praying after the window closes (must make up later).
